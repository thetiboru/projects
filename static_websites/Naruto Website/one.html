History of Naruto

Storyline

The Original Naruto story had the main character Naruto being a chef. The story was tweaked and he became a boy who could transform into a fox. Ultimatley both these storylines were scrapped and the show became about ninjas. The storyline of Naruto is primarily based on Japanese culture, including popular Japanese tales like the Nine-Tailed Fox. Author Kishimoto also made use of the Chinese zodiac tradition when developing some of the hand signs frequently used in the show.

Characters

The characters from Naruto originate from Japanese culture. Some characters are named after Japanese words, for instance, character Itachi's name means weasel, and Kakashi means scarecrow. Some of the other characters originating from Japanese culture are the three legendary sanin, also known as Jiraiya, Orochimaru, and Tsunade. These characters don't have last names as they are named after characters from Japanese mythology.

Inspirations

Kishimoto admits that a lot of the inspiration from Naruto came from the Japanese anime known as Hunter x Hunter. Togashi, the creator of Hunter x Hunter, was good friends with Kishimoto and they shared a lot of ideas. Some of the characters Kishimoto took inspiration from were Sasuke, Orochimaru, Kakashi, and Madara.
